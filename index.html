Itinerary

Sat 6
Arrival — settle in & Amalfi town
Easy arrival day
▼
Afternoon
Check in & terrace time
Settle in and open the first bottle of chilled Falanghina on your private terrace. The view down to the port is the best possible start.
Ask hotel to pre-stock minibar with prosecco & Amalfi lemon granita
5:00 pm
Stroll through Amalfi town
Take the hotel shuttle down. Wander Piazza del Duomo, step inside the Cathedral of Sant'Andrea and its stunning 9th-century bronze doors, and explore the Chiostro del Paradiso cloister.
6:30 pm
Aperitivo — hotel cliffside bar
The hotel's cliffside bar at sunset is one of the most spectacular views on the coast. Order a Limoncello Spritz and let the evening arrive slowly.
8:00 pm
Dinner — La Locanda by Gino Sorbillo
The hotel's pizza and Mediterranean restaurant. Authentic Neapolitan pizza from one of Naples' most celebrated pizza makers, with that coastal backdrop. No need to go anywhere.
📋 Book a terrace table at check-in
Late
Jacuzzi under the stars
The terrace jacuzzi with the lights of Amalfi below and the Tyrrhenian Sea beyond. Pour something cold and don't rush to bed.
Sun 7
Private boat to Positano — beaches & shopping
The big boat day
▼
7:30 am
Breakfast on the panoramic terrace
The lavish hotel buffet terrace — one of the best breakfasts on the coast. Go early for a quiet table and the best light.
9:30 am
Private boat departs — Amalfi port
Private gozzo or motorboat west along the coast to Positano with swimming stops in hidden coves en route. Prosecco and snacks included on most private charters.
📋 Book ahead: KissFromItaly, Lucibello or Positano By Boat. ~€400–600 for a full day private charter. Summer books out weeks ahead.
12:00 pm
Fornillo beach, Positano
Ask the skipper to drop you at Fornillo — quieter than Spiaggia Grande, with excellent small restaurants and a genuinely relaxed atmosphere. Hire sunbeds from one of the beach clubs.
1:30 pm
Lunch — Da Vincenzo or Saraceno d'Oro
Both are family-run, highly rated, and far better value than tourist-facing spots near Spiaggia Grande. Fresh seafood pasta, grilled octopus, cold white wine.
3:00 pm
Shopping — Via dei Mulini & backstreets
Custom handmade leather sandals made to measure at Safari Sandals or Artigianato Rallo. Linen at La Bottega di Brunella. Ceramics and jewellery tucked into stairway boutiques.
Wander the side steps for best artisan quality — avoid the main road tourist shops
5:00 pm
Sunset aperitivo — Li Galli Bistro
Extraordinary views of the coast for a pre-departure Aperol Spritz. Watch the light change over the Faraglioni rocks.
6:30 pm
Boat back to Amalfi at dusk
Watch the cliffside villages light up on the return. Ask your skipper to take it slowly.
8:30 pm
Dinner — Ristorante Marina Grande
Right on the Amalfi waterfront. Lobster pasta, seafood risotto, excellent local wine. Consistently top-rated for food, location, and romance.
📋 Book ahead — request a terrace sea-view table
Mon 8
Ravello & Michelin star dinner
The most romantic day
▼
9:00 am
Lazy morning — room service breakfast
Breakfast on your private terrace, or the hotel buffet. A late, relaxed start.
11:00 am
Taxi to Ravello
Just 4 miles but a world apart. Take a taxi (~€20–30) rather than the twisty bus. Arrive before the day-tripper coaches for the villas almost to yourselves.
11:30 am
Villa Cimbrone — Terrace of Infinity
One of the most beautiful places in Italy. Walk through 11th-century gardens — roses, hydrangeas, marble temples — to the Terrazza dell'Infinito, 365m above the Mediterranean. Gore Vidal called the view from here the most beautiful in the world. He wasn't wrong.
Go straight to the terrace when it opens, then explore the gardens on the way back
1:00 pm
Lunch — Cumpa' Cosimo, Ravello
A beloved family-run trattoria on Via Roma. Handmade pasta, roasted meats, warm welcome. The antidote to hotel fine dining at lunchtime — excellent value.
2:30 pm
Villa Rufolo & Piazza Duomo
The 13th-century villa's Moorish cloister, terraced gardens and coastal panorama. Then wander Ravello's quiet streets, browse ceramics shops, and sit in the square with a coffee.
5:00 pm
Return to hotel — Anantara Spa
Book a couples treatment in advance. Then the terrace jacuzzi for pre-dinner drinks.
📋 Book spa treatment at check-in — gets busy quickly
7:30 pm
★ Dinner — Rossellinis, Palazzo Avino
The finest dinner on the Amalfi Coast. Michelin-starred. Champagne in the garden terrace at dusk, then dinner inside — Chef Giovanni Vanacore's light regional cooking, an extraordinary wine list, views down to the coast. Tasting menus named poetically ("La Mia Storia con Ravello"). Allow 3–4 hours. Budget ~€400–500 for two with wine.
📋 Book direct: info@palazzoavino.com · +39 089 818181. Ask for garden terrace welcome & Sommelier's Table if available.
Tue 9
Capri by private boat
Full day at sea
▼
8:30 am
Private boat to Capri — Amalfi port
Fully private ~8 hour charter. The crossing passes Li Galli Islands (where the Sirens of Greek myth supposedly lived) with swimming stops before reaching Capri.
📋 Book: KissFromItaly (kissfromitaly.com) — private charter direct from Amalfi port
11:00 am
Blue Grotto & Faraglioni rocks
The Blue Grotto — neon blue light through a submerged limestone cave (~€15 extra per person by rowboat, worth it). Then the Faraglioni sea stacks and Marina Piccola bay for swimming in extraordinary water.
Only accessible when seas are calm — your skipper will know on the day
1:00 pm
Lunch — Nerano village or Capri town
Nerano bay is famous for Spaghetti alla Nerano — the courgette and provola pasta dish invented here. Or take the funicular up to Capri town for something more glamorous.
3:00 pm
Capri town — the Piazzetta
One of the world's great people-watching spots. Carthusia perfumers (the iconic local fragrance house), ceramics, limoncello, high fashion. Coffee at a Piazzetta table watching the world go by.
5:00 pm
Sunset cruise back to Amalfi
Ask your skipper to time the return for the golden hour. A final swim off the cliffs of Praiano or Furore. Most private charters open prosecco for the return leg.
8:00 pm
Dinner — Dei Cappuccini, Anantara hotel
After a long day at sea, eat at the hotel's fine dining restaurant. Chef Claudio Lanuto's contemporary Amalfi Coast menu in a 13th-century Capuchin monastery dining room. Unforgettable setting.
📋 Book at check-in — after Capri you won't want to go far
Wed 10
Slow morning & departure
Gentle final day
▼
Morning
Final breakfast & infinity pool
The Anantara's infinity pool is cut directly into the clifftop rock — one of the most dramatic on the Amalfi Coast. Spend the morning here before checkout.
11:00 am
Lido degli Artisti beach club
The hotel offers access just 5 minutes away by boat. Arrange the night before. One last few hours of Tyrrhenian sun.
1:00 pm
Final lunch — Il Tari or Da Gemma
Both are long-established Amalfi classics on or near the main piazza. One last plate of seafood pasta and a cold glass of local white.
3:00 pm
Final wander & limoncello shopping
Amalfi's Sfusato lemons are unique to this coast. Pick up artisan limoncello from Andrea Pansa pasticceria. The Museo della Carta (paper museum) is a hidden 15-minute gem if time allows.

Notes & tips

Info
Practical notes
Bookings, budget, tips
▼
Rossellinis Book immediately: info@palazzoavino.com or +39 089 818181. Ask for the garden terrace welcome and enquire about the Sommelier's Table (private cave, 2 guests).
Boat charters Book both private boat days as soon as possible — September charters sell out. KissFromItaly for Capri; Lucibello or Positano By Boat for the Positano day.
Hotel shuttle Free shuttle to Amalfi town every 30 mins. For Ravello, taxi is ~€20–30 — far easier than the bus.
Wine to order Ask for Falanghina, Fiano di Avellino, or Greco di Tufo (local white wines). For red: Aglianico del Taurasi. All Campanian and exceptional.
Blue Grotto Only accessible in calm seas. Your skipper decides on the day — don't bank on it but it's worth trying.
Dress code Amalfi is smart-casual throughout. Rossellinis: smart-casual minimum, jacket recommended. Capri Piazzetta: linen and slightly elevated fits the scene.
Budget guide Private boat (per day): €400–600. Rossellinis dinner for two: ~€400–500 with wine. Beach clubs: €20–30/person. Capri funicular: €2.50 each way. Villa Cimbrone entry: €7/person.
